Module 4:
Structure
Here we’ll study the structure of an article through and through!
It’s time we learn the structure of an article on the example brought to us by Mr Octopus!

Task:

Write an article about your favourite things about the Internet. Talk about the things you do with the Internet and recommend a website to other readers.
Articles must always have a title. If possible, this must be catchy and witty so as to engage the reader. Each paragraph must address a single idea, and your article must have the introduction and the conclusion, where you are usually expected to make a recommendation. In most articles we have subheadings for each paragraph, which makes it easier to comprehend & look through to get the main ideas. It is also a good idea to end your article with a rhetorical question, especially if it is going to be an online article where people could (in theory) leave a comment.
    In order to write a well-structured article, make sure to:
  1. make the structure clear, define an introduction and conclusion;
  2. have a clear objective (idea) for each paragraph;
  3. work on the introduction: it must present the topic in a general way, but make clear what your article is about;
  4. work on the conclusion: it is a summary that often asks the reader for some kind of response.
